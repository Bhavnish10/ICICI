INVESTMENT THESIS  
ICICI Bank is positioned as a high -quality growth stock  due to its robust fundamentals. In FY2024 it earned ₹408.88 billion PAT , 
which rose to ₹472.27 billion in FY2025  (≈+15%)  [1]. Its return on equity (~17 –18%)  [6][7] is industry -leading. The bank has 
rapidly expanded retail lending and digital services, fuelling  double -digit growth. It trades at ~17× forward earnings and 2.7× book 
value  [6], which is reasonable given its sustained 18% -plus ROE  [7]. I expect ICICI's annualized return to exceed 15% over 3 –
5 years , driven by continued loan growth and improving margins . 
 
BANKING SECTOR OUTLOOK  
• Strong Credit Growth: RBI reports non -food bank credit up ~14.4% YoY in Dec 2025  [4], reflecting rising demand from 
retail consumers, SMEs, and infrastructure projects.  
• Healthy Bank Balance Sheets: Indian banks saw double -digit loan and deposit growth in FY2025  [8], with gross NPAs at 
multi -year lows (~2.1% as of Sept 2025  [5]). ICICI's own GNPA stands at ~1.67% in Mar 2025  [9]. 
• Digital & Regulatory Tailwinds: Financial inclusion efforts and digital adoption are expanding the customer base. Private 
banks like ICICI continue gaining market share through superior technology and efficiency.  
 
COMPANY ANALYSIS  
• Profitability: Core operating profit grew 12.5% in FY2025 (₹653.96 bn)  [10]. NII surged to ₹73,240 cr (FY2024)  [2], with 
NIM at 4.4–4.5%  [11][12], among the highest in the sector. Loan book grew +13.9% YoY in Q4 FY2025.  
• Asset Quality: GNPA declined from 2.29% (FY2024) to 1.67% in Q4 FY2025  [9]; PCR at ~84%  [14]. Best -in-class asset 
quality limits credit costs and adds material safety margin.  
• Capital & Returns: CET1 of ~17.7% (vs 11.5% regulatory minimum). ROE at 18.4% in FY2024  — a level that justifies 
current multiples and enables lending growth without dilution. [15] [7] 
• Digital / Retail Leadership: 30M+ iMobile Pay users; 93% of transactions digital; retail loans >55% of advances. Digital 
penetration drives higher -yield retail exposure and deep customer stickiness. [16][17] 
• Corporate Governance: No promoter concentration; dispersed institutional ownership. MD Sandeep Bakhshi's term extended 
— management continuity is a significant differentiator vs. peers.  
 
GROWTH DRIVERS  
• Retail Credit Expansion: Rising incomes and urbanisation sustain housing, auto, and personal loan demand. Digital -first 
platforms (iLens, InstaBIZ) provide scalable origination.  
• SME & Corporate Lending: Government focus on MSMEs and infrastructure creates a multi -year lending runway. ICICI's 
balance sheet strength positions it as a preferred lender.  
• Net Interest Margin Resilience: Stable CASA (~39%) keeps funding costs low. As new loan yields outpace deposit repricing, 
NIM may expand from current ~4.4%. [11] 
• Fee Income Diversification: Credit cards, insurance distribution, and wealth management are growing rapidly, reducing 
dependence on interest income.  
• Subsidiary Synergies: Dividends and cross -sell from ICICI Prudential, ICICI Lombard, and ICICI Securities bolster group 
earnings stability and ROE.  
 
 
KEY RISKS  
Risk Factor  Description  
Economic Slowdown  A sharp growth deceleration could dent loan volumes and elevate NPA provisions, particularly in 
unsecured retail and SME portfolios.  
Asset Quality Shocks  RBI audit (FY2026) caused a one -off provision spike. Any large corporate default could pressure 
GNPA ratios above current lows.  
Regulatory Changes  Higher capital requirements, revised priority -sector norms, or fee caps could constrain loan 
growth or compress fee income.  
Interest Rate Volatility  Rapid rate cuts could compress NIM before the loan book is repriced; rapid hikes could stress 
unsecured borrowers.  
Competitive Pressure  Fintech entrants and aggressive private peers compete in cards, personal loans, and SME lending, 
potentially pressuring yields.

CONCLUSION  
ICICI Bank's strong fundamentals — double -digit profit growth, best -in-class ROE, and declining NPAs — combined with India's 
structurally favourable  banking environment make it a compelling long -term investment. It operates a well -capitalised, digital -first 
franchise with growing fee income streams and subsidiary optionality. Trading at a reasonable premium to book for its return profile, 
the stock is  expected to generate >15% annual returns  over the next 3 –5 years. ICICI Bank is recommended as a BUY.
